Home Buyer Tips

Did You Know?

Tap each card to reveal the answer.

01

How much should you really have saved before buying a home?

Most buyers focus only on the down payment, but you also need 2–5% of the purchase price for closing costs, plus 3–6 months of mortgage payments in reserves. A common goal: save at least 10–15% of your target home price before starting your search.

02

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is a quick estimate based on self-reported info — it holds little weight with sellers. Pre-approval is a verified lender review of your income, credit, and assets. In a competitive Houston market, always come with a pre-approval letter before making offers.

03

Does a higher offer always win in a multiple-offer situation?

Not always! Sellers also weigh closing timeline, contingencies, and financing type. An all-cash offer or fewer contingencies can beat a higher bid. Your agent's ability to communicate your offer's strengths to the listing agent can make all the difference.

04

Should you skip the home inspection to make your offer more attractive?

Almost never. A home inspection typically costs $300–$500 and can uncover issues worth tens of thousands. Instead, consider an information-only inspection — you waive the right to negotiate repairs but still know what you're buying. Never skip it entirely.

05

What hidden costs do first-time buyers often forget about?

Beyond the mortgage: property taxes, HOA fees, homeowner's insurance, PMI (if down payment < 20%), utilities, and ongoing maintenance (budget ~1% of home value/year). In Houston, flood insurance is also worth considering depending on the zone.

06

How long does buying a home in Houston typically take?

From starting your search to closing, expect 2–4 months on average. Getting pre-approved takes a few days, finding the right home varies, and once under contract the closing process is typically 30–45 days. Starting early — even just exploring — puts you ahead.
